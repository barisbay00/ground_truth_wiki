Core definitions

The smallest unit we will be dealing with is a pixel. A pixel is one addressable square of an image.

A binary image is an image where each pixel belongs to one of two classes, foreground (black) or and background (white) in our case.

Binarization is the process of converting an image into a binary image.

A ground truth is the reference that other outputs will be judged against.

For our purposes, a ground truth is a manually corrected binary image that is the ideal reference to what should be black ink and what should be white background based on the original image.
