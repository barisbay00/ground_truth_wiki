Core Concepts

Ground Truth

A ground truth is simply a trusted reference that can be used to evaluate (or train) an algorithm. A popular example you might have heard of concerns LLMs: in supervised learning, LLMs train on data that often includes ground-truth labels. In simple terms, it is given a picture of a cat and the ground truth label of the image is "cat". The model makes a prediction and then compares it to the ground truth. It then measures the error and adjusts itself to reduce that error over many of such examples. Eventually, it learns to predict the correct answer.

Now, we WILL NOT be training any LLMs and the pipeline does not make use of any machine learning. But, the concept is more or less similiar: In this project, the ground truth is the final manually corrected binary image. It is will be used to test whether a binarization algorithm correctly separates foreground from background. Based on the results, we will also be able to tweak things.

What ground truth means here

The ground truth should answer one question:

For each pixel, should this be foreground (imagine ink, black) or background (imagine white, page)?

The answer must be based on the original image, not on the binarized draft.

Why it matters

Binarization algorithms are compared against the ground truth. A clean ground truth lets us measure the accuracy of the algorithm. If the ground truth is wrong, the test result is wrong. Plus, binarization feeds the rest of the pipeline, so everything else will also be wrong. As a result, forests might burn, earthquakes, tsunamis, and the like... No, of course not. Luckily, linguistics does not come with too much at stake.

Some important points

A ground truth is not many things: it is not a beautified version of the page. it is not a general cleanup where the editor changes layout, size, rotation, or content it does not reconstruct what the text probably said

A good ground truth has these properties: It is binary, so black and white only (no other color or shades). It preserves visible meaningful marks. It features less noise. It has the same image dimensions as the provided draft. It can be tied back to the original image by its name. It has notes for uncertain cases.
