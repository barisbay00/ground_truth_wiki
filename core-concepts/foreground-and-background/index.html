Core Concepts

Foreground and Background

Simply, foreground is the meaningful visible content that the binary image should preserve and background is everything else.

Foreground examples

  • text
  • punctuation
  • diacritics

Default background examples

  • paper texture
  • scanner shadows
  • dust
  • random speckles
  • bleed-through from the other side
  • stains, unless they are meaningful marks
  • margins and empty page areas

Ambiguous cases

When uncertain, compare the original image and draft. If still uncertain, follow Ambiguity Handling and record the case in your notes.
